5.2 Types of Advertisements

Our mobile applications may display the following types of advertisements:

  • Splash Ads: Full-screen advertisements displayed during app launch or loading. These ads appear automatically and typically last 3-5 seconds before users can dismiss them.
  • Interstitial Ads: Full-screen advertisements displayed at natural transition points in the app, such as between game levels, after completing an action, or when switching screens. Users can typically close these ads after a countdown period.
  • Rewarded Video Ads: Full-screen video advertisements that users voluntarily watch in exchange for in-app rewards, such as virtual currency, bonus content, or feature unlocks. Users must actively opt-in to watch these ads.
  • Banner Ads: Rectangular advertisements displayed at the top or bottom of the app screen. These ads continuously display throughout the app session and may refresh periodically.
  • Native Ads: Advertisements designed to match the visual style and format of the app's content, appearing as sponsored content within the app's regular content flow.
  • Playable Ads: Interactive advertisements that allow users to try a brief demo of an app or game before downloading.
  • Offerwall Ads: Advertisements presenting multiple offers or tasks that users can complete for rewards.

5.4 Your Choices Regarding Advertising

You can control personalized advertising on your device:

  • Android: Go to Settings > Google > Ads > Opt out of personalized advertising
  • iOS: Go to Settings > Privacy > Apple Advertising > View Ad Targeting Information > Turn off Personalized Ads
  • App Tracking Transparency: On iOS 14.5+, you can control whether apps can track your activity across other companies' apps and websites
  • Reset Advertising IDs: You can reset your device's advertising identifier at any time
  • Limit Ad Tracking: Enable this setting to restrict the use of your advertising identifier

8. Regional Privacy Regulations Compliance

8.1 European Union (GDPR)

If you are located in the European Union, you have the following rights under the General Data Protection Regulation (GDPR):

  • Right to access your personal data
  • Right to rectification of inaccurate data
  • Right to erasure ("right to be forgotten")
  • Right to restrict processing
  • Right to data portability
  • Right to object to processing
  • Right to withdraw consent
  • Right to lodge a complaint with a supervisory authority

We process personal data based on: consent, contract performance, legal obligations, vital interests, public tasks, and legitimate interests.

8.2 California Consumer Privacy Act (CCPA/CPRA)

If you are a California resident, you have the following rights under the California Consumer Privacy Act and California Privacy Rights Act:

  • Right to know what personal information is collected
  • Right to know how personal information is used and shared
  • Right to delete personal information
  • Right to opt-out of the sale or sharing of personal information
  • Right to correct inaccurate personal information
  • Right to limit the use of sensitive personal information
  • Right to non-discrimination for exercising your rights

We do not sell personal information in the traditional sense. However, we may share certain information with advertising partners, which may be considered "sharing" under CCPA.

8.3 United Kingdom (UK GDPR)

Users in the United Kingdom have rights similar to those under EU GDPR, including rights to access, rectification, erasure, and the right to lodge complaints with the Information Commissioner's Office (ICO).

8.4 Canada (PIPEDA)

Users in Canada have rights under the Personal Information Protection and Electronic Documents Act (PIPEDA), including the right to access, correct, and withdraw consent for the use of their personal information.

8.5 Australia (Privacy Act 1988)

Users in Australia have rights under the Australian Privacy Act, including the right to access and correct personal information, and the right to make complaints about privacy breaches.

8.6 Brazil (LGPD)

Users in Brazil have rights under the Lei Geral de Proteção de Dados (LGPD), including rights to confirmation of data processing, access to data, correction of incomplete or inaccurate data, anonymization, blocking, or deletion of unnecessary data.

8.7 Japan (APPI)

Users in Japan have rights under the Act on Protection of Personal Information (APPI), including rights to disclosure, correction, cessation of use, and deletion of personal information.

8.8 South Korea (PIPA)

Users in South Korea have rights under the Personal Information Protection Act (PIPA), including rights to access, correct, delete, and suspend processing of personal information.

8.9 China (PIPL)

Users in China have rights under the Personal Information Protection Law (PIPL), including rights to know, decide on, and restrict processing of personal information, as well as rights to access, copy, correct, and delete personal information.

8.10 India (PDP Bill)

Users in India have rights under India's data protection framework (Digital Personal Data Protection Act 2023), including rights to access information, correction, erasure, grievance redressal, and the right to nominate.
